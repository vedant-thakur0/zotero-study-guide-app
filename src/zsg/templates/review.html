How this works

You annotate in Zotero. The AI drafts. You review and approve. Nothing reaches students until you say so.

  1. Setup — Paste your API key and confirm the AI model. Optionally rename what each highlight color means for your course.
  2. Pipeline — Create a project, export your Zotero annotations as HTML, and upload the file. The app parses highlights, groups them into sections, and calls the AI to write first drafts.
  3. Narrative Review — Read each section side-by-side with your source highlights. Edit freely, then click Approve when you're satisfied. Nothing is exported until every section you want is approved.
  4. Quiz Review — For approved narrative sections, generate quiz questions from your red (Quiz-worthy) highlights. Review, edit, and approve each set.
  5. Export — Choose a title, theme, and navigation style, then click Build Guide. You get a single self-contained HTML file ready to share with students.

The AI never touches your final guide without your review. Every section and quiz set must be explicitly approved before it is included in the export.
